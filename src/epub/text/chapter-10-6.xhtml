The superior man did not use a deep purple, or a puce colour, in the ornaments of his dress.667

Even in his undress, he did not wear anything of a red or reddish colour.668

In warm weather, he had a single garment either of coarse or fine texture, but he wore it displayed over an inner garment.669

Over lamb's fur he wore a garment of black; over fawn's fur one of white; and over fox's fur one of yellow.670

The fur robe of his undress was long, with the right sleeve short.671

He required his sleeping dress to be half as long again as his body.672

When staying at home, he used thick furs of the fox or the badger.673

When he put off mourning, he wore all the appendages of the girdle.674

His under-garment, except when it was required to be of the curtain shape, was made of silk cut narrow above and wide below.675

He did not wear lamb's fur or a black cap, on a visit of condolence.676

On the first day of the month he put on his court robes, and presented himself at court.677
