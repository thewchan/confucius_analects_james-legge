XXIV776

Tzǔ-lu got Tzǔ-kao appointed governor of Pi.777

The Master said, "You are injuring a man's son."778

Tzǔ-lu said, "There are (there) common people and officers; there are the altars of the spirits of the land and grain. Why must one read books before he can be considered to have learned?"779

The Master said, "It is on this account that I hate your glib-tongued people."780
