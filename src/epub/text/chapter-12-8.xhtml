Chi Tzǔ-chʽêng said, "In a superior man it is only the substantial qualities which are wanted;—why should we seek for ornamental accomplishments?"811

Tzǔ-kung said, "Alas! Your words, sir, show you to be a superior man, but four horses cannot overtake the tongue.812

Ornament is as substance; substance is as ornament. The hide of a tiger or a leopard stripped of its hair, is like the hide of a dog or a goat stripped of its hair."813
