Tzǔ-lu said, "The Duke Huan caused his brother Chʽiu to be killed, when Shao Hu died with his master, but Kuan Chung did not die. May not I say that he was wanting in virtue?"972

The Master said, "The Duke Huan assembled all the princes together, and that not with weapons of war and chariots:—it was all through the influence of Kuan Chung. Whose beneficence was like his? Whose beneficence was like his?"973
