Chʽên Chʽêng murdered the Duke Chien of Chʽi.986

Confucius bathed, went to court, and informed the duke Ai, saying, "Chʽên Hêng has slain his sovereign. I beg that you will undertake to punish him."987

The duke said, "Inform the chiefs of the three families of it."988

Confucius retired, and said, "Following in the rear of the great officers, I did not dare not to represent such a matter, and my prince says, 'Inform the chiefs of the three families of it.'"989

He went to the chiefs, and informed them, but they would not act. Confucius then said, "Following in the rear of the great officers, I did not dare not to represent such a matter."990
