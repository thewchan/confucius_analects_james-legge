VIII1286

The men who have retired to privacy from the world have been Po-yi, Shu-chʽi, Yü-chung, Yi-i, Chu-chang, Hui of Liu-hsia, and Shao-lien.1287

The Master said, "Refusing to surrender their wills, or to submit to any taint in their persons;—such, I think, were Po-yi and Shu-chʽi.

"It may be said of Hui of Liu-hsia, and of Shao-lien, that they surrendered their wills, and submitted to taint in their persons, but their words corresponded with reason, and their actions were such as men are anxious to see. This is all that is to be remarked in them.1288

"It may be said of Yü-chung and Yi-i, that, while they hid themselves in their seclusion, they gave a license to their words; but, in their persons, they succeeded in preserving their purity, and, in their retirement, they acted according to the exigency of the times.1289

"I am different from all these. I have no course for which I am predetermined, and no course against which I am predetermined."1290
