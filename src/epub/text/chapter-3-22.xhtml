The Master said, "Small indeed was the capacity of Kuan Chung!"233

Some one said, "Was Kuan Chung parsimonious?"

"Kuan," was the reply, "had the San Kuei, and his officers performed no double duties; how can he be considered parsimonious?"234

"Then, did Kuan Chung know the rules of propriety?"

The Master said, "The princes of states have a screen intercepting the view at their gates. Kuan had likewise a screen at his gate.235 The princes of states on any friendly meeting between two of them, had a stand on which to place their inverted cups. Kuan had also such a stand. If Kuan knew the rules of propriety, who does not know them?"236
