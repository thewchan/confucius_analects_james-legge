Mêng Wu< asked about Tzŭ-lu, whether he was perfectly virtuous. The Master said, "I do not know."a href="endnotes.xhtml#note-319" id="noteref-319" epub:type="noteref">319

He asked again, when the Master replied, "In a kingdom of a thousand chariots, Yu might be employed to manage the military levies, but I do not know whether he be perfectly virtuous."320

"And what do you say of Chʽiu?" The Master replied, "In a city of a thousand families, or a clan of a hundred chariots, Chʽiu might be employed as governor, but I do not know whether he is perfectly virtuous."322

"What do you say of Chʽih?" The Master replied, "With his sash girt and standing in a court, Chʽih might be employed to converse with the visitors and guests, but I do not know whether he is perfectly virtuous."324
