Chi Kʽang asked about Chung-yu, whether he was fit to be employed as an officer of government. The Master said, "Yu is a man of decision; what difficulty would he find in being an officer of government?" Kʽang asked, "Is Tzʽŭ fit to be employed as an officer of government?" and was answered, "Tzʽŭ is a man of intelligence; what difficulty would he find in being an officer of government?" And to the same question about Chʽiu the Master gave the same reply, saying, "Chʽiu is a man of various ability."395
