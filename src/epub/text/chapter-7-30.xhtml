The minister of crime of Chʽên asked whether the duke Chao knew propriety, and Confucius said, "He knew propriety."511

Confucius having retired, the minister bowed to Wu-ma Chʽi to come forward, and said, "I have heard that the superior man is not a partisan. May the superior man be a partisan also? The prince married a daughter of the house of Wu, of the same surname with himself, and called her,—'The elder Tzû of Wu.' If the prince knew propriety, who does not know it?"512

Wu-ma Chʽi reported these remarks, and the Master said, "I am fortunate! If I have any errors, people are sure to know them."513
