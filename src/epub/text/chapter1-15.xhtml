XV82

Tzŭ-kung said, "What do you pronounce concerning the poor man who yet does not flatter, and the rich man who is not proud?"83 The Master replied, "They will do; but they are not equal84 to him, who, though poor, is yet cheerful, and to him, who, though rich, loves the rules of propriety."

Tzŭ-kung replied, "It is said in the Book of Poetry, 'As you cut and then file, as you carve and then polish.'85—The meaning is the same, I apprehend, as that which you have just expressed."86

The Master said, "With one like Tzʽŭ,87 I can begin88 to talk about the odes. I told him one point, and he knew its proper sequence."89
