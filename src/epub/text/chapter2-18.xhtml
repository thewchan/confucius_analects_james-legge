Tzŭ-chang151 was learning with a view to official emolument.152

The Master said, "Hear much and put aside the points of which you stand in doubt,153 while you speak cautiously at the same time of the others:—then you will afford few occasions for blame. See much and put aside the things which seem perilous, while you are cautious at the same time in carrying the others into practice:—then you will have few occasions for repentance. When one gives few occasions for blame in his words, and few occasions for repentance in his conduct, he is in the way to get emolument."154
