VIII318

Mêng Wu319 asked about Tzŭ-lu, whether he was perfectly virtuous. The Master said, "I do not know."

He asked again, when the Master replied, "In a kingdom of a thousand chariots,320 Yu might be employed to manage the military levies,321 but I do not know whether he be perfectly virtuous."

"And what do you say of Chʽiu?"322 The Master replied, "In a city of a thousand families, or a clan of a hundred chariots, Chʽiu might be employed as governor, but I do not know whether he is perfectly virtuous."323

"What do you say of Chʽih?"324 The Master replied, "With his sash girt and standing in a court, Chʽih might be employed to converse with the visitors and guests, but I do not know whether he is perfectly virtuous."325
